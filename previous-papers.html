ANSThe SI unit of electrical resistance is the ohm, represented by the symbol omega.

ANSOhm's law states that current through a conductor is directly proportional to the voltage across it, provided temperature remains constant: V = IR.

ANSElectrical power P equals voltage multiplied by current, P = VI, and can also be expressed as I squared R or V squared divided by R.

ANSKCL states that the total current entering a junction equals the total current leaving that junction, based on conservation of charge.

ANSKVL states that the sum of all voltage drops around any closed loop in a circuit equals zero, based on conservation of energy.

ANSElectrical energy for billing purposes is measured in kilowatt-hour (kWh), commonly called a unit of electricity.

ANSA transformer works on the principle of mutual electromagnetic induction, transferring energy between coils through a changing magnetic flux.

ANSThe turns ratio represents the ratio of primary to secondary winding turns, which determines the voltage step-up or step-down factor.

ANSSlip is the difference between synchronous speed and rotor speed, expressed as a percentage of synchronous speed.

ANSSynchronous speed Ns equals 120 times the supply frequency divided by the number of poles (Ns = 120f/P).

ANSDC generators are classified as separately excited and self-excited, with self-excited further divided into shunt, series and compound types.

ANSThe commutator converts alternating current generated in the armature into direct current output, acting as a mechanical rectifier.

ANSPower factor is the cosine of the phase angle between voltage and current, indicating how effectively electrical power is being used.

ANSA low power factor increases current for the same real power delivered, causing higher losses and requiring larger conductors and equipment.

ANSA circuit breaker automatically interrupts current flow during fault conditions to protect equipment and prevent electrical hazards.

ANSA fuse melts and must be replaced after operating, while a circuit breaker can be reset and reused after tripping.

ANSEarthing provides a safe path for fault current to flow into the ground, protecting equipment and people from electric shock.

ANSThe standard AC supply frequency in India is 50 Hertz.

ANSThree-phase supply consists of three alternating currents of the same frequency, offset by 120 degrees, commonly used for industrial power transmission.

ANSThree-phase supply delivers more power with smaller conductors, provides constant power flow, and is more efficient for large industrial loads.

ANSHT stands for High Tension (high voltage) and LT stands for Low Tension (low voltage) supply categories.

ANSIt steps down high voltage from the distribution feeder to a lower voltage suitable for direct consumer use.

ANSTransmission losses refer to the electrical energy lost as heat while power travels through transmission lines, mainly due to conductor resistance.

ANSTransmitting at high voltage reduces current for a given power level, which significantly lowers resistive losses (I squared R losses) in the lines.

ANSA step-up transformer increases voltage from the generating station level to a higher transmission voltage to minimise transmission losses.

ANSA step-down transformer reduces high transmission voltage to a safer level suitable for distribution and end-user consumption.

ANSA relay detects abnormal conditions like faults and signals circuit breakers to isolate the affected section of the network.

ANSA short circuit fault occurs when a low-resistance path forms unintentionally between conductors, causing very high current flow.

ANSAn open circuit fault occurs when a break in the conducting path stops current flow entirely through that path.

ANSInsulators support conductors on towers while preventing current leakage to the grounded tower structure.

ANSCorona effect is the ionisation of air around a high-voltage conductor causing a faint glow and associated power loss, especially in humid conditions.

ANSLoad factor is the ratio of average load to maximum demand over a specific period, indicating how efficiently capacity is utilised.

ANSDemand factor is the ratio of maximum demand to total connected load, showing how much of the installed capacity is actually used at peak.

ANSDiversity factor is the ratio of the sum of individual maximum demands to the coincident maximum demand of the whole system.

ANSA synchronous generator converts mechanical energy into AC electrical energy, running at a speed synchronised with the supply frequency and pole count.

ANSExcitation refers to supplying DC current to the rotor field winding to create the magnetic field needed for voltage generation.

ANSA voltage regulator maintains the generator's output voltage at a set level despite variations in load.

ANSThe per unit system expresses electrical quantities as fractions of chosen base values, simplifying calculations across different voltage levels.

ANSA bus bar is a common conducting point where multiple incoming and outgoing circuits connect within a substation.

ANSAC (alternating current) periodically reverses direction, while DC (direct current) flows in a single constant direction.

ANSAC can be easily stepped up or down using transformers, making it more efficient for transmitting power over long distances.

ANSAn isolator provides visible physical disconnection of a circuit for maintenance, but should only be operated when no load current is flowing.

ANSTariff refers to the rate structure used to calculate the amount consumers pay based on their electricity consumption category and usage.

ANSA two-part tariff includes a fixed charge based on maximum demand or connected load, plus a variable charge based on actual energy consumed.

ANSMaximum demand is the highest average power demand recorded over a specified short interval during a billing period.

ANSAn energy meter records the total electrical energy consumed by a connection over time, typically in kilowatt-hours.

ANSIt uses the interaction between alternating magnetic fluxes and induced eddy currents in an aluminium disc, causing it to rotate proportional to energy consumed.

ANSFault level indicates the maximum short-circuit current that could flow at a given point, used to size protective equipment adequately.

ANSProtective relays are broadly classified as electromechanical, static, and numerical (digital) relays based on their operating technology.

ANSGrid frequency regulation balances generation and demand in real time to keep system frequency close to its nominal value, such as 50 Hz.

ANSA capacitor bank improves power factor by supplying reactive power locally, reducing losses and improving voltage stability.

ANSReactive power is the power that oscillates between source and load without doing real work, measured in kilovolt-ampere reactive (kVAR).

ANSApparent power is the combination of real and reactive power, measured in kilovolt-amperes (kVA), representing total power supplied to a circuit.

ANSApparent power squared equals the sum of real power squared and reactive power squared, forming the power triangle relationship.

ANSIt quickly detects and isolates faults occurring on the busbar itself to prevent widespread outage across connected circuits.

ANSFavourable wind corridors in southern Tamil Nadu districts encouraged early and sustained investment in wind turbine installations over recent decades.

ANSA lightning arrester protects equipment by diverting high-voltage surges from lightning strikes safely to the ground.

ANSIt measures the resistance of insulation material in cables and equipment to check for degradation and prevent leakage current risks.

ANSA megger is an instrument used to measure high insulation resistance values, helping detect insulation faults in cables and windings.

ANSActive energy meters record real energy consumed (kWh) doing useful work, while reactive energy meters record reactive energy (kVARh) associated with inductive or capacitive loads.
